Equality and identity

In the first post of this series, we gave a programming-inspired motivation of type theory and gave some basic definitions and examples. However, to enjoy the full power of dependent type theory (and its extensions) we have to introduce the most important type of them all: the identity type. The natural deduction rules are the following ones:
\begin{gather} A:\type\\ \hline a,a':A\vdash a=_Aa':\type \end{gather}
\begin{gather} A:\type\\ \hline a:A\vdash\mathrm{refl}_A(a):a=_Aa \end{gather}
The introduction rules says that for every term $a:A$ there always exists a proof of the equality $a=a$. The notation reflects that it is a canonical proof of the reflexivity of equality. (The formation rule also says that the identity types can be combined into a single dependent type.)
\begin{gather} a,a':A,p:a=_Aa'\vdash C(a,a',p):\type\quad a:A\vdash t:C\big(a,a,\mathrm{refl}_A(a)\big)\\ \hline a,a':A,p:a=_Aa'\vdash J(t;a,a',p):C(a,a',p) \end{gather}
\begin{gather} a,a':A,p:a=_Aa'\vdash C(a,a',p):\type\quad a:A\vdash t:C\big(a,a,\mathrm{refl}_A(a)\big)\\ \hline a:A\vdash q(a):J\big(t;a,a,\mathrm{refl}_A(a)\big)=_{C\big(a,a,\mathrm{refl}_A(a)\big)}t. \end{gather}
The elimination rule says that if we have a type depending on the proofs of equality and if for every reflexivity proof we also have an explicit term, then we can transport this term along the proof. This idea of transporting terms will play an important role in the geometric interpretation of type theory. (If in the computation rule, one replaces the existence of an equality proof of $t$ and $J\big(t;a,a,\mathrm{refl}_A(a)\big)$ by this being a judgemental equality, the notion of strong identity types is obtained.)
